Environmental Impacts

Threshold, Samantha Schwann

Scattered around the ocean are areas of such significance, that their protection is scientifically recognized with the ability to impact global ocean health.

Our Warming World, Daniel Beltra

My project asks us to consider the landscape as a place we have altered, all while striving to coexist within the natural world.

New Foundland Global Warming: Climate Change and the Fishing Industry Impact, Paul Marotta

During the mid 1500s, salted, preserved cod caught in the North Atlantic accounted for more than half of all the fish eaten in Europe as Portugal, Spain, France and Britain put enormous pressure on cod stocks.

Social Justice

Knife Fight City and The Kingdom of Dust, Richard Street

Knife Fight City explores an unacknowledged variety of American apartheid in Huron, poorest town in California, where an American peasantry slaves for industrialized agriculture in a giant farm labor exploitation camp.

Shadow Lives, USA, Jon Lowenstein

Shadow Lives USA is a decade-long project that humanizes, in a highly intimate fashion, the experiences and lives of the millions of men and women who leave their homes in search of a better life in the United States.

Seven Doors, Greg Constantine

After spending the past twelve years working on the project Nowhere People, this next phase of the project expands the work to use photography, multimedia and other forms of visual storytelling to expose the impact of immigration detention on stateless people as well as migrants, refugees and asylum seekers around the world.

Indigenous Cultures and Displacement

Katutubong Pilipino, Jacob Maentz

Project Katutubong Pilipino is a long-term documentary project with the aim of showcasing the beauty and depth of Philippine indigenous cultures and highlighting the many environmental and social issues these groups are currently facing.

Indigenous Autonomy in Mexico, Scott Brennan

The photographic project focuses on the coastal town/municipality of Santa Maria de Ostula on the violent and largely undeveloped coast of Michoacan in southern Mexico.

The Remote Arctic Town that is Melting Away, Anna Filipova

As the Arctic loses ice and the permafrost is thawing at dramatic rates, people in Greenland, are finding their homes, livelihoods, customs and very survival at risk.
